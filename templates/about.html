In the late 1980s, Ted Turner's cable TV group bought movie studio MGM's archive, which in addition to feature films also included many cartoons from Warner Bros. In 1991 Turner also bought animation studio Hanna-Barbera Productions and got their large archive included in the purchase. In order to have space for all these films, the cable channel The Cartoon Network was created. [2] The channel was initially specialized in showing reprints of classic classic films such as the Flinta family. Ted Turner's company Turner Broadcasting was purchased in 1996 by media giant Time Warner. Cartoon Network then became part of Time Warner.
